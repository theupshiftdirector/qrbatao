QR Codes for Business — 10 Ways to Grow

Practical ways businesses of all sizes are using QR codes in 2026 — from contactless payments and digital menus to inventory management and customer loyalty programs.

Updated: March 2026

1. Contactless Payments

UPI QR codes have transformed payments in India. Every shop, street vendor, and business can accept digital payments instantly by displaying a simple QR code. Customers scan with any UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM) and pay directly from their bank account. No card machines, no cash handling, no transaction fees for most UPI payments.

  • For retail shops: Display a UPI QR code at the billing counter. Instant payment confirmation via notification.
  • For online businesses: Include your UPI QR code on invoices, emails, and order confirmation pages.
  • For freelancers: Share your payment QR code via WhatsApp or email for easy invoice settlement.

2. Digital Menus for Restaurants

QR code menus became standard during the pandemic and are here to stay. Instead of printed menus that need frequent reprinting, a QR code on each table links to a digital menu that can be updated instantly — change prices, add seasonal items, mark items as sold out, and add photos.

  • Place QR codes on table tents, coasters, or stickers on each table.
  • Link to a mobile-friendly menu page (Google Sites, Canva, or your own website).
  • Update the menu anytime without any printing costs.
  • Add allergen information, nutritional details, and ingredient lists digitally.

3. Marketing and Promotions

QR codes bridge the gap between offline and online marketing. Print a QR code on flyers, posters, newspaper ads, or billboards to drive customers directly to your website, landing page, social media profile, or promotional offer.

  • Print ads to website: Add QR codes to newspaper ads, magazine ads, and hoardings. Track offline-to-online conversions using UTM parameters.
  • Social media growth: Print QR codes linking to your Instagram, YouTube, or WhatsApp Business profile on packaging, receipts, and shopping bags.
  • Coupons and discounts: Encode coupon codes or link to discount pages. Place on receipts, packaging, or in-store displays.
  • App downloads: Link directly to your app's Play Store or App Store page from physical marketing materials.

4. Customer Feedback and Reviews

Collecting customer feedback is crucial for business growth. A QR code on the receipt, table, or packaging can link directly to a Google Reviews page, a feedback form, or a survey — making it effortless for customers to share their experience.

  • Link to your Google Business Profile review page for instant reviews.
  • Connect to a Google Form or Typeform survey for detailed feedback.
  • Place QR codes on packaging inserts to collect post-purchase feedback.
  • Offer a small incentive (discount on next purchase) for completing the survey.

5. Inventory and Asset Management

QR codes are a cost-effective alternative to RFID tags for tracking inventory, equipment, and assets. Each item gets a unique QR code that links to its details in your inventory management system.

  • Warehouse tracking: Label each shelf, bin, or pallet with a QR code. Workers scan to update stock levels in real-time.
  • Equipment management: Tag office equipment (laptops, projectors, furniture) with QR codes linking to maintenance history and assignment details.
  • Supply chain: Track packages from warehouse to delivery using QR codes at each checkpoint.

6. Loyalty Programs

Replace physical loyalty cards with QR code-based digital loyalty. Customers scan a QR code at each purchase to earn points, stamps, or rewards — no card to lose, no app to download.

  • Create a simple Google Form-based check-in system where customers scan and enter their phone number.
  • Use loyalty platforms (like Reelo, Kangaroo, or Square Loyalty) that provide QR-based tracking.
  • Display the loyalty QR code at checkout with clear instructions on how to earn and redeem rewards.

7. Product Packaging and Information

QR codes on product packaging provide customers with detailed information without cluttering the label. Link to user manuals, how-to videos, ingredient details, warranty registration, or authenticity verification.

  • User manuals: Replace bulky printed manuals with a QR code linking to a digital version (PDF or video).
  • Product authentication: Let customers verify product authenticity by scanning a unique QR code on the packaging.
  • Nutritional information: Food products can link to detailed nutritional data and allergen information beyond what fits on the label.
  • Warranty registration: Scan to register the product for warranty, eliminating paperwork and making claims easier.

8. WiFi Sharing for Customers

Offering free WiFi is a customer magnet for cafes, restaurants, co-working spaces, and hotels. A WiFi QR code lets customers connect instantly by scanning — no need to ask for the password or type it manually.

  • Create a WiFi QR code encoding your network name (SSID) and password.
  • Display it prominently at the entrance, tables, or reception area.
  • When you change the WiFi password, simply generate a new QR code and replace the display.

9. Event Management

QR codes streamline event management from registration to check-in to post-event follow-up.

  • Ticketing: Issue digital tickets with unique QR codes. Scan at entry for quick, contactless check-in.
  • Registration: Place QR codes on posters and invitations linking to the registration form.
  • Networking: Attendee badges with QR codes encoding contact details (vCard). Scan to exchange contacts instead of handing out business cards.
  • Feedback: Display QR codes at the exit or on seats linking to a post-event survey.

10. Hiring and Recruitment

Businesses are using QR codes to simplify the hiring process. A QR code on a "We're Hiring" poster or store window links directly to the job application form or careers page.

  • Place QR codes on in-store "Now Hiring" signs linking to an online application form.
  • Add to job fair booths for instant resume submission.
  • Include on business cards of recruiters linking to open positions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to create a QR code for business?

Basic static QR codes are completely free to generate using tools like QR Batao. For most small businesses, free static QR codes are sufficient for payments (UPI), website links, WiFi sharing, and contact sharing. Dynamic QR codes with tracking and editing capabilities typically cost $5-50/month depending on the provider and features needed.

What size should a QR code be for print materials?

The minimum recommended size for a printed QR code is 2 x 2 cm (about 0.8 x 0.8 inches) for close-range scanning (business cards, receipts). For posters and signage scanned from a distance, follow the 10:1 rule — the QR code should be at least 1/10th of the expected scanning distance. For a poster viewed from 1 meter away, the code should be at least 10 cm wide.

Can QR codes work without internet?

It depends on the content type. QR codes encoding text, phone numbers, WiFi credentials, or vCard data work completely offline — the data is read directly from the code. QR codes encoding URLs require internet to open the website. UPI payment QR codes require internet to process the payment through the banking network.

Are QR codes secure for business payments?

Yes, UPI QR codes for payments are secure when generated correctly. The QR code only contains the payee's UPI ID and payment amount — no sensitive banking data is exposed. The actual payment is processed through the secure UPI network (managed by NPCI). However, businesses should protect their QR codes from being tampered with or replaced by fraudsters.

How long do QR codes last?

Static QR codes last forever — they have no expiration date. The code will work as long as the encoded content remains valid (e.g., the website URL is active). Dynamic QR codes depend on the service provider's server and may stop working if the subscription expires. For permanent business use, static QR codes are more reliable.
