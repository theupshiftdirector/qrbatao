UPI QR Code Guide — Create, Scan & Accept Payments

Everything you need to know about UPI QR codes in India — how to create one, the difference between personal and merchant QR codes, transaction limits, and how to stay safe.

Updated: March 2026

What Is a UPI QR Code?

A UPI QR code is a two-dimensional barcode that encodes UPI (Unified Payments Interface) payment information, allowing anyone to make instant bank-to-bank payments by simply scanning the code with any UPI-enabled app. When scanned, the QR code pre-fills the payee's UPI ID and optionally the payment amount, so the payer just needs to confirm and enter their UPI PIN.

UPI QR codes follow the BharatQR and UPI QR standards defined by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India). They are interoperable — a QR code generated from Google Pay works with PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, and every other UPI app. This interoperability is what makes UPI QR codes the backbone of India's digital payment revolution.

As of 2026, over 300 million merchants in India accept payments via UPI QR codes, from large retailers to street vendors. UPI processes over 10 billion transactions per month, with QR code-based payments forming a significant share.

How to Verify a QR Code Is Safe

QR code fraud (especially in UPI payments) is a growing concern. Follow these safety practices:

  • Always verify the payee name: After scanning a UPI QR code, your app displays the payee's name and UPI ID. Verify this matches the intended recipient before proceeding. If the name looks suspicious or doesn't match, do not proceed.
  • Never enter UPI PIN to "receive" money: This is the most common UPI fraud. Your UPI PIN is only needed to send money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to receive a payment, it is a scam.
  • Check for tampered QR codes: At shops and payment points, look for stickers pasted over original QR codes. Fraudsters replace merchant QR codes with their own to divert payments. If a QR code looks like a sticker placed on top, ask the merchant to verify.
  • Do not scan QR codes from unknown sources: Be cautious of QR codes received via WhatsApp, SMS, email, or social media from unknown senders. These may lead to phishing pages that look like your bank's website.
  • Use collect request verification: If you receive a "collect request" (payment request) via UPI after scanning a QR code, verify the amount and requester carefully. Decline requests you did not initiate.
  • Report fraud immediately: If you fall victim to UPI fraud, report it immediately to your bank, the NPCI helpline (18001201740), and file an FIR. UPI fraud complaints can also be filed on the RBI's CPGRAMS portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create a UPI QR code for my business?

You can create a UPI QR code for your business in three ways: (1) Use QR Batao's free UPI QR code generator — enter your UPI ID, business name, and optionally a fixed amount, then download the QR code image. (2) Generate one from your UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) under the "Receive Money" or "My QR Code" section. (3) Apply for a merchant QR code from your bank or payment service provider for transaction tracking and settlement features.

What is the difference between a personal and merchant UPI QR code?

A personal UPI QR code is linked to your personal bank account and UPI ID (like yourname@upi). It has a daily receiving limit of Rs 1 lakh and no transaction tracking features. A merchant UPI QR code is linked to a business account, offers a higher daily limit (Rs 2 lakh or more), provides transaction reports, daily settlement summaries, and may offer features like Paytm Soundbox or PhonePe SmartSpeaker for instant payment confirmation.

What is the UPI QR code daily transaction limit?

For personal UPI accounts, the daily transaction limit is Rs 1 lakh (combined sending and receiving). For merchant UPI accounts, the limit is typically Rs 2 lakh per transaction with no daily cap on the number of transactions. Certain categories like tax payments, education fees, and hospital payments support up to Rs 5 lakh per transaction. These limits are set by NPCI and may be updated periodically.

How can I verify if a UPI QR code is safe to scan?

Before scanning a UPI QR code: (1) Check that the QR code is not a sticker pasted over the original one. (2) After scanning, verify the payee name and UPI ID displayed on your screen matches the intended recipient. (3) Never enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money — UPI PIN is only needed to send money. (4) For online purchases, scan QR codes only from the official website or app. (5) Be wary of QR codes received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email from unknown senders.

Can I create a UPI QR code with a fixed amount?

Yes, you can create a UPI QR code with a pre-filled fixed amount. When someone scans this QR code, the amount field is automatically populated and they just need to confirm and enter their UPI PIN. This is useful for fixed-price products, subscription payments, or donation collection. You can generate fixed-amount UPI QR codes using QR Batao's UPI QR code generator for free.
