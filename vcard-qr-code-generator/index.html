How to Create a vCard QR Code

A vCard QR code encodes your contact information in the standard vCard format (VCF). When someone scans it with their phone camera, a prompt appears to add your details to their phone contacts — name, phone number, email, company, and website in one tap.

vCard QR codes are the modern replacement for paper business cards. Print them on your actual business card, add them to your email signature, display them at conferences, or put them on your desk nameplate.

Fill in your details above and click Generate. All processing happens in your browser — your contact information is never sent to any server.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when someone scans my vCard QR code?

Their phone displays a "Add to Contacts" prompt with all your pre-filled details — name, phone, email, company, website. One tap saves everything to their contacts.

Which fields are required?

Only a first or last name is required. All other fields (phone, email, organization, title, website) are optional. Include whatever information you want to share.

Can I put a vCard QR code on my business card?

Yes, that's one of the most popular uses. Download the SVG version for best print quality. The QR code is typically placed on the back of the business card.

Does it work on both iPhone and Android?

Yes. Both iOS and Android natively support vCard format. When scanned, both platforms offer to add the contact details directly to the phone's address book.

What's the difference between vCard and a URL QR code linking to a contact page?

A vCard QR code saves contact info directly to the phone — no internet needed. A URL QR code opens a webpage, which requires internet but can display richer information like a profile photo or social links.
